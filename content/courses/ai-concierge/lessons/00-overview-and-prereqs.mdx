This course is for a developer with a working shop — Next.js (or any framework, the principles transfer), a Postgres backed catalogue, a logged-in user model behind a JWT — who needs to ship a customer-facing AI concierge in about a month. Not a chatbot demo. A concierge that the founder shows on the homepage, that real customers ask real questions of, that drafts real orders. The course is what the team would put together if they were teaching themselves the path.

## 01 — What this course assumes

You know JavaScript or TypeScript at the level of *"I can read and modify an async function"*. You know SQL at the level of *"I can write a join and a WHERE clause"*. You have a shop with at least a product catalogue and a user model — bonus if you have order history, but the course shows you how to bootstrap recommendations without it.

You do **not** need:

- Prior LLM experience.
- A data science background.
- Vercel, Next.js, or any specific framework — code samples use Next.js for the chat endpoint and Node for scripts, but the patterns translate.
- A separate vector database account. (You don't need one — this course uses `pgvector` on the Postgres you already have.)

## 02 — The foundations course this one builds on

There is a sister course on this site called **AI in Your Project**. It teaches the **general** LLM-integration toolkit: streaming, prompts, tool calling, multi-turn loops, LangGraph, the `interrupt()` confirmation pattern, safety, testing, observability, and the production swap from a local Ollama to a hosted provider.

This course **does not re-teach those topics**. Instead, where they matter, this course points at the specific lesson in **AI in Your Project** that covers it. If you've worked through Parts I-V of that course, you're ready for this one. If you haven't, you can read selectively as you go — every lesson here that depends on foundations links to the exact lesson you'd want to read first.

The natural pairing is **Part 6 of AI in Your Project** — *Building a Customer-Facing Concierge*. Those eight lessons are the deep technical content; this course is the orientation and the build path through them.

<Callout type="tip" title="The fastest way to study">
If you have a free afternoon, read Parts I-V of *AI in Your Project* as scanning — you don't need to retain every detail, just see the shape. Then come back here for the concierge-specific architecture and quickstart. Most developers spend 2-3 hours on the scan and feel ready to start building.
</Callout>

## 03 — The four-week timeline

The course is paced for **four weeks of focused work**, roughly 2 hours/day or 10 hours/week. The shape:

| Week | Goal                                                | This course                      | Foundations to read                            |
| ---- | --------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | Chat skeleton + FAQ retrieval working end-to-end    | L02 quickstart                   | AIYP L25 (RAG vs Tools), L26 (pgvector)        |
| 2    | Read tools (orders, stock, profile) wired in        | L03 reading guide                | AIYP L09-11 (tools), L27 (hybrid retrieval), L28 (session/auth) |
| 3    | Write tools (orders, booking, email) + confirmation | L03 reading guide                | AIYP L12-19 (writes, idempotency, interrupt), L29 (recommend) |
| 4    | Cost routing, deploy to VPS, soft-launch            | L04 going-live checklist         | AIYP L21-24 (safety, test, observe, prod), L30 (gateway), L31 (deploy) |

The week-one goal is on purpose modest. By the end of day five you have a Postgres + Next.js stack that answers *"chính sách giao hàng là gì?"* using your own FAQ markdown. Nothing else. That single working flow is the foundation everything else hangs from — if you skip ahead and try to do all three at once, you spend a week debugging the wrong layer.

## 04 — How this course is structured

Five lessons total. Two orientation, two build, one ship.

**L00 — Overview and prerequisites** (this one). Sets expectations and the foundations map.

**L01 — The concierge architecture.** One diagram, the whole system: chat endpoint, retrieval, tools, write gate, audit log, gateway. Names the boundaries so the rest of the course has a vocabulary.

**L02 — Week one quickstart.** A code-along that takes you from `docker run pgvector` to a working FAQ-answering bot in a few hours. The point isn't to be production-ready; it's to feel the loop work end-to-end before adding any complexity.

**L03 — From foundations to concierge.** A reading guide through AIYP Part 6 for weeks 2-3 — what to read, in what order, with what application to your concierge. Not new technical content; orientation through dense material.

**L04 — Going-live checklist.** The final inventory: soft-launch ramp, monitoring loop, kill switches, runbook. The list of things that have to be true before flipping the rollout knob to 100%.

**Project — Your shop's concierge.** Not a canned project — it's *your* concierge, configured to your shop's domain. The course's deliverable is yours, on your VPS, serving your customers.

## 05 — Bilingual throughout

The course is written in English and Vietnamese in parallel. Code samples and configuration are language-neutral; explanatory prose, system prompts, and dataset examples appear in both. The concierge you build is bilingual by default — customers in HCMC can chat in Vietnamese, customers abroad in English, and the same backend handles both.

The Vietnamese half is **not** machine-translated. It's written from scratch in Vietnamese with the same technical accuracy as the English. Vocabulary choices favour what a working developer in Vietnam would use — sometimes that's an English loanword (*"endpoint"*, *"deploy"*, *"cache"*), sometimes it's the Vietnamese term where it's more natural (*"truy hồi"* for retrieval, *"cổng xác nhận"* for confirmation gate).

<Callout type="note" title="If your shop is monolingual">
You can ignore one of the two locales entirely. The schema still works; you just only ingest one language's FAQ. The session-locale logic short-circuits. The cost is roughly half — you're not embedding two corpora. Nothing about the rest of the build depends on bilingual.
</Callout>

## 04 — Course này cấu trúc thế nào

Tổng năm bài. Hai orientation, hai build, một ship.

**L00 — Tổng quan và prerequisites** (bài này). Set kỳ vọng và bản đồ foundations.

**L01 — Kiến trúc concierge.** Một sơ đồ, cả hệ thống: endpoint chat, retrieval, tool, cổng write, audit log, gateway. Đặt tên cho các biên để phần còn lại của course có vocabulary.

**L02 — Quickstart tuần một.** Code-along đưa bạn từ `docker run pgvector` đến bot trả lời FAQ chạy được trong vài giờ. Mục đích không phải production-ready; mà là cảm nhận vòng lặp chạy end-to-end trước khi thêm phức tạp.

**L03 — Từ foundations đến concierge.** Reading guide qua AIYP Phần 6 cho tuần 2-3 — đọc gì, theo thứ tự nào, với áp dụng nào vào concierge bạn. Không phải nội dung kỹ thuật mới; orientation qua tài liệu nặng.

**L04 — Checklist going-live.** Kiểm kê cuối: ramp soft-launch, vòng monitoring, kill switch, runbook. Danh sách những thứ phải đúng trước khi vặn núm rollout lên 100%.

**Project — Concierge của shop bạn.** Không phải project đóng hộp — là concierge *của bạn*, config cho domain shop bạn. Sản phẩm bạn giao của course là của bạn, trên VPS bạn, phục vụ khách bạn.

## 05 — Song ngữ xuyên suốt

Course viết song song Anh và Việt. Code mẫu và config trung lập ngôn ngữ; văn xuôi giải thích, system prompt, và ví dụ dataset xuất hiện cả hai. Concierge bạn dựng mặc định song ngữ — khách HCM chat tiếng Việt, khách nước ngoài tiếng Anh, và cùng backend xử lý cả hai.

Nửa tiếng Việt **không** dịch máy. Nó viết từ đầu bằng tiếng Việt với cùng độ chính xác kỹ thuật như tiếng Anh. Lựa chọn từ ngữ thiên về cái developer làm việc ở Việt Nam sẽ dùng — đôi khi là loanword tiếng Anh (*"endpoint"*, *"deploy"*, *"cache"*), đôi khi là từ tiếng Việt khi tự nhiên hơn (*"truy hồi"* cho retrieval, *"cổng xác nhận"* cho confirmation gate).

<Callout type="note" title="Nếu shop bạn đơn ngữ">
Bạn có thể bỏ qua một trong hai locale hoàn toàn. Schema vẫn chạy; bạn chỉ ingest FAQ một ngôn ngữ. Logic locale session short-circuit. Chi phí khoảng một nửa — bạn không embed hai corpus. Không gì khác trong phần build phụ thuộc vào song ngữ.
</Callout>
